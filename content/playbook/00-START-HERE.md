# The Teen Business Playbook

This is the exact process Jay Bhakta used to build Outsmart the System, a financial education business for teens and families, turned into steps you can follow with no technical skills. Nothing in here is theory. Every method, every template, and every mistake comes from the real build: the brand interview, the offers, the website, the emails, the content, the automation, the scoreboard, and the rules that keep all of it honest. You do not need to know how to code. You need to be able to read, write, talk to real people, and keep your word.

Credit where it is due: the StoryBrand framework is Donald Miller's. The content direction is James Guldan's. The money-story material is Tom Farrar's. The reliability idea is Nic Peterson's. This playbook teaches you to credit your sources the same way.

## How to use this playbook

- Read the chapters in order the first time. Each one builds on the last. After that, jump around as needed.
- Each chapter ends with your move: a worksheet or starter plan that turns the chapter into something you actually built. Do the move before you read the next chapter.
- Expect the whole build to take weeks, not a weekend. Jay's build took months. Slow and real beats fast and fake.
- A parent or guardian needs to be in the loop for anything involving money or accounts: the domain, Stripe, terms and agreements, and any signup that asks for payment details.

## The method: CASE

One simple order runs underneath the whole playbook. It is called CASE: Compile, Analyze, Strategize, Execute.

- **Compile:** gather the truth first. Go talk to real people and write down their exact words. That is Chapter 1, Market Research.
- **Analyze:** turn those raw words into a brand. Sort what people said into who your customer is, what hurts, and what they want.
- **Strategize:** design the offer, then make it a no-brainer (this takes two chapters), build the website, and set up your follow-up, all aimed at what you learned.
- **Execute:** put it into the world with content every week, and keep a scoreboard that tells you what is working.

Older versions of this playbook skipped the first step and opened with Analyze. They started by building a brand and quietly assumed you already knew your customer's exact words. Now the playbook opens with Compile, because you cannot analyze what you have not gathered. Do not treat CASE as a rigid law. Treat it as the order the work naturally wants to go: get the truth, make sense of it, plan around it, then ship.

## The toolbox

Every tool this playbook mentions, in one line each. You do not need any of them on day one. Each chapter tells you when a tool enters and what to do instead if you skip it.

- **An AI assistant (like Claude):** a chat program that can interview you, draft your pages and posts, and check your work. It forgets everything between sessions, which is why chapter 12 exists. You stay the editor.
- **StoryBrand:** not a tool, a framework. Donald Miller's method for describing your business as a story where your customer is the hero and you are the guide. Chapter 2 walks you through it.
- **A website page builder:** a tool that turns words into web pages without code. Any beginner builder works. Chapter 5 shows the process, not a brand.
- **GoHighLevel, or any email tool:** software that keeps every contact in one list and sends emails automatically. Jay uses GoHighLevel. Any email tool with tags and automations does the job.
- **Stripe:** a company that processes online payments and gives you a ready-made checkout page you can share as a link. Parent in the loop to set it up.
- **PostEverywhere, or any scheduler:** an app that publishes your loaded posts to your accounts at set times, so posting runs itself while you show up for replies.
- **HeyGen:** software that makes videos of your real self speaking from typed text. Used only under the real-self-only rules in chapter 9. Never a cartoon version of you.
- **Substack, or any long-form home:** a free platform for publishing weekly essays that also emails them to your subscribers.
- **A spreadsheet:** any spreadsheet works for your weekly scoreboard. Start here. Chapter 10 explains the fancier tools, and why you do not need them yet.

## Master glossary

Every term from every chapter, in one alphabetical list. Each chapter also defines its own terms as they appear.

- **/go counting doorway:** routing every link you publish through one countable hop on your own site, so every click gets counted.
- **301 redirect:** a permanent "we moved" sign so old links reach the new page instead of an error.
- **4-event model:** the only four numbers that matter: clicks, leads, paid, revenue. Every measurement maps to one.
- **A/B test:** showing two versions (like two prices) to different people and letting the results pick the winner.
- **Abandoned checkout:** someone clicked buy but did not finish; a friendly reminder goes out a few hours later.
- **Acceptance proof:** the observable evidence that a step actually worked, so "done" is something you can see, not something you feel.
- **Active listening:** asking a question, then staying silent so the other person fills the space with the real answer. The awkward silence is where the truth lives.
- **Additive (build alongside) / additive shipping:** the rule for changing things that work: build the new next to the old instead of rewriting the old, so a launch cannot break what already works. "Untouched" is verified, not assumed.
- **Adversarial verification (red team):** attacking your own work with a skeptic pass before trusting it. You only act on what survives.
- **After-payment redirect:** the page a buyer lands on right after paying. Set it in the Payment Link settings so it points at your welcome page, not a bare receipt.
- **AI avatar:** software that makes a video of you speaking from typed text, using a scan of your face and a clone of your voice. Rule: your real self or nothing.
- **AI clipping:** uploading one long video and letting AI find and cut the best short moments.
- **Anchor:** the library item (story, belief, or framework) a post is built on. One anchor per post.
- **Anonymized:** a story told with every identifying detail removed.
- **Attribution:** naming the person whose idea you borrowed. Free, and it builds relationships instead of resentment.
- **Audit:** a structured, honest inspection of what you have built. It outputs a findings list you verify, then fix one item at a time.
- **Automation / workflow:** a robot recipe: when the trigger fires, do these steps in order.
- **B-roll:** background footage that plays under a voiceover.
- **Back-end:** a premium offer sold only to people who already bought, never to strangers.
- **Baseline:** your real starting numbers, written down before you change anything, so progress is provable.
- **Batch / batching:** doing all of one kind of work in one sitting, like making a whole week of content at once.
- **The bleed:** the ongoing problem that quietly costs your customer money, sleep, or peace, day after day, the thing that never quite gets fixed. People pay to stop a bleed.
- **Bonus:** an extra added on top of your offer stack that answers one specific objection from your objection bucket. Must be real and something you actually deliver.
- **Brain dump:** a recorded ramble about your week. The raw material every post starts from.
- **Brainstorm prompt:** a question you keep on hand to jog a memory loose when the story file page is blank.
- **Broadcast:** one email sent manually to many people at once.
- **Burned captions:** subtitles printed into the video itself, so they show with the sound off.
- **Cache-busted check:** reloading a page with a random `?x=123` on the URL so you see the new version, not a saved old copy.
- **CASE:** the method this playbook runs on: Compile, Analyze, Strategize, Execute. Gather the truth first, then build on it in that order. You cannot analyze what you have not compiled.
- **Claims policy:** your list of banned claim types (unproven counts, unprovable partnerships, clinical language).
- **Click:** someone tapped your link. Interest, proven by a thumb.
- **Compile:** gathering real words from real people instead of inventing them. The first step of the work and the job of the market research chapter.
- **Compliance line:** the plain sentence on your page that says what you are and are not ("education, not financial advice").
- **Confessional voice:** first person, real, mess included. The register that works, per James Guldan's direction. The opposite of lecture voice.
- **Consent:** written permission, collected before you publish, not after.
- **Content seed:** a raw idea pulled from a brain dump that is not a post yet.
- **Conversion:** the percentage of people who see an ask and take it.
- **Copy:** the marketing word for the written words on your pages, posts, and emails.
- **CRM:** software that keeps your contact list and what each person has done, with tags that trigger the right follow-up. A contacts app with superpowers.
- **CTA (call to action):** the one thing a post, page, or email asks the reader to do. Direct means buy. Transitional means take a free smaller step first.
- **Dated ruling:** a rule change written down with its date and reason. Rules never change silently.
- **Decision gate:** a date on the calendar, set in advance, where you look at the numbers and make a pre-written call instead of drifting.
- **Defensible value:** a value number you can stand behind out loud, either what you truly charge for that piece or a real comparable price. Anything else is a fabricated stat and is banned from your offer stack.
- **Deliverability:** whether your emails actually reach inboxes instead of spam.
- **Deliverable:** a concrete thing the buyer receives that you can point at: a roadmap, a kit, a session.
- **Dinner-party test:** say your one-liner to someone cold; if they get it and ask a follow-up question, it passes.
- **Do-it-yourself / done-with-you / done-for-you:** the effort spectrum for an offer. The more you do with or for the buyer, the lower their effort and the higher the value.
- **Domain:** your rented address on the internet, like `yourbusiness.com`.
- **Do-not-touch list:** the written list of working, load-bearing things the AI may not modify during a change. Written before the work starts, checked by you after it ends.
- **Draft:** a post loaded into the scheduler but not yet live; you review in draft, then approve.
- **Dream Outcome:** the result the customer deeply wants, stated in their own words and pulled from your dream bucket. A top lever of the Value Equation, pushed up.
- **Effort and Sacrifice:** how hard, confusing, or annoying an offer is for the buyer. A bottom lever of the Value Equation, pushed down by doing the hard parts for them or with them.
- **End card:** a short branded closing screen with your name and your ask.
- **Entry offer:** the small paid product that proves you are worth paying.
- **External / internal / philosophical problem:** what is visibly going wrong / how it makes the customer feel privately / why the situation is just plain wrong in the first place.
- **Fabricate (fabrication, hallucination):** to make something up and present it as fact, whether you did it or your AI did. The one unforgivable move in this business.
- **Faceless video:** video with no one on camera; cards, footage, or motion graphics plus a voiceover.
- **Fast first win:** a small, real result the buyer gets early (the first session or first days) that raises belief and momentum at once, lowering the felt time delay.
- **First-hour replies:** answering every comment within an hour of posting. The conversion engine. Never automated.
- **Fit:** whether a person actually matches who your offer is for, scored separately from how much you like them.
- **Flagship:** your main program, where the real transformation and real revenue live.
- **Follow-up:** the one (and only one) polite second message you send after silence. It must add something new, never a guilt trip.
- **Front door:** the first thing a stranger is asked to do, usually free and quick (a quiz, a check, a calculator). It gets the one countable link.
- **Funnel:** the path a stranger walks from finding you to paying you.
- **GA4 (Google Analytics 4):** Google's free dashboard that stores click tallies for a website. It only counts from the day you set it up.
- **The gap:** the difference between your honest total value and your price. The bigger and truer the gap, the easier the yes.
- **Grand Slam Offer (no-brainer offer):** an offer so clearly worth more than its price that the right person feels silly saying no. The thing you build in the no-brainer chapter, from Alex Hormozi's "$100M Offers" (2021).
- **Guide:** the role your business plays in the customer's story. Empathy first, authority second.
- **Hero (composite):** one realistic made-up person who stands for your typical customer, blended from real people you actually talked to.
- **Hook:** the first line of a post, or the first 1.5 seconds of a video. Its only job is to stop the scroll.
- **Hooks bank:** your file of first lines that already worked, reused before inventing new ones.
- **Hosting:** the computer that keeps your page on the internet 24/7. Beginner page builders usually include it.
- **Human gate:** the rule that nothing customer-facing ships without a human reading and approving it. In your business, that human is you.
- **ICP (ideal customer profile):** the exact kind of customer your offer is built for. An ICP gate checks fit before checkout.
- **Ken Burns effect:** slowly zooming or panning on a still image so it feels like video.
- **Keyword DM / keyword post:** "comment WORD and I'll send it," turning a public comment into a private conversation you handle personally, never with a bot on a personal profile.
- **Kill/scale rules:** thresholds you write before starting that say when to stop something and when to double down, so feelings do not decide.
- **Kit credit:** money spent on your small offer counts toward your big one, so nobody pays twice.
- **Landing page:** one page about one offer with one main button.
- **Lead:** a person who gave you their email but has not paid yet. A raised hand you can follow up with.
- **Lead magnet:** the free useful thing you trade for an email address.
- **Library:** the folder holding your BrandScript, voice memo, and story file. Every future post traces back to something in it.
- **Link in bio:** the workaround for platforms where caption links are not clickable; the caption points to the link on your profile.
- **Load-bearing:** a part your business rests on, like the payment link or the live page. Named for the wall that holds a building up. Load-bearing parts go on the do-not-touch list.
- **Magic wand question:** asking a customer to describe their perfect solution ("if I could wave a magic wand..."), so they hand you your feature list in their own words.
- **Market research:** talking to real potential customers to learn how they think, feel, and speak, before you build anything.
- **Memory file:** your running log of decisions, gotchas, and open items, read at the start of every AI session so nothing gets re-argued.
- **Merge field:** a placeholder like [first name] that the email tool fills in per person.
- **Named job (skill):** a repeated job written down once as a named, versioned checklist file (like WEEKLY-RUN) that the AI executes, instead of instructions retyped from memory every week.
- **Nurture sequence:** pre-written emails that go out automatically over days, warming a lead toward one offer.
- **Objection:** the real reason someone did not buy: too expensive, wrong time, does not trust it yet, does not need it.
- **Objection log:** the table where every no is recorded with a date, a category, and the person's exact words.
- **Offer:** the deal you put in front of someone: what they get, what it costs.
- **Offer ladder:** your offers arranged cheap to expensive so trust can build in steps.
- **Offer stack:** the full bundle listed in one place, each item with an honest, defensible value, a real total, and a price set below that total.
- **Onboarding email:** the instant "you're in, here's step 1-2-3" email after a purchase.
- **One-liner:** your business in one spoken sentence, built from the who / failure / success formula.
- **Operator voice:** short sentences, plain words, no hype. The voice of someone doing the work, not selling the dream.
- **Opt-in page:** a page whose only job is getting a visitor to type in their email, opting in to hear from you.
- **Orders document:** a numbered instruction file written in one AI session and executed in a later one, with exact steps and proof of done for each.
- **Orphan page:** a page nothing links to, which therefore gets no visitors.
- **Outreach:** contacting one specific, named person to ask them one specific thing, instead of posting and hoping.
- **Packet:** one document holding a whole week's long piece, short cuts, and publish instructions, ready to paste.
- **Page builder:** a tool that turns words into web pages without code.
- **Paper trading:** practicing investing with pretend money before any real dollar moves.
- **Parked:** the honest label for a section you cannot fill in yet. The opposite of making something up.
- **Payment link:** a ready-made checkout page from Stripe; you paste the URL behind your button. No coding.
- **Perceived Likelihood:** how sure the buyer is that the offer will actually work for THEM, not just for someone in an ad. A top lever of the Value Equation, raised only by honest proof.
- **Pipeline:** a board showing how far each person has traveled, from signup to customer.
- **Pivot:** a deliberate, dated change of direction based on evidence, logged in your truth file.
- **Polarizing belief:** a true opinion you hold that filters in the right customers and filters out the wrong ones.
- **Preflight / dry run:** checking every caption, link, and claim as if posting, before anything real goes out.
- **Price anchor:** a real number a real customer named when asked what they would pay. It becomes the starting point for your offer, instead of a price you guessed.
- **Price to value:** setting your price against the value you deliver, not against your costs or the cheapest competitor. Too cheap can signal low value and starve the work.
- **Privacy page:** one honest paragraph saying what you collect, what you do with it, and that you never sell it.
- **Probe:** a short follow-up ("How so?", "Can you tell me more?", "How did that make you feel?") that hands the talking back to the customer without putting words in their mouth.
- **Prospect:** a specific person you plan to ask, with a name and a way to reach them.
- **Pseudonymize:** change names, ages, places, and dollar amounts in real stories so nobody is identifiable. Keep only the emotional truth.
- **Publish gate:** a checklist that can block a post. If the post fails a check, it does not go out until fixed.
- **Quality gates:** the fixed checklist (banned words, specificity, hook, no reruns, read-aloud, never invent) every piece must pass.
- **Recording consent:** clear permission, asked for before you hit record, every time. Rules vary by place, so you always ask.
- **Recurring billing:** a charge that repeats automatically; if your offer does this, say so in plain words on the page.
- **Redirector:** a short link you control that counts the click, then forwards the visitor to the real page.
- **Reference id (`client_reference_id`):** a name tag on a payment link so your systems know which product was bought, even when two cost the same.
- **Refund promise (guarantee):** your written, specific rule for when money comes back.
- **Release form (consent release):** the signed written permission you need before telling a real person's story publicly. Under 18 requires both the teen's and a parent's signature.
- **Research call:** a short, honest conversation (about 15 minutes) where you interview a potential customer to collect their exact words. A research call is not a sales call.
- **Review gate:** the human read-and-approve step before anything publishes. AI drafts, a human ships. See also human gate.
- **Revision trigger:** a written "redo this when X happens" rule, so documents get refreshed on evidence, not vibes.
- **Risk reversal (guarantee):** moving the risk of "what if it doesn't work?" off the buyer and onto you. Types: unconditional, conditional (do-the-work), and all-sales-final. Never guarantees a result you cannot control, and never a money or performance outcome.
- **Rotation log (anti-repeat ledger):** the running record of which anchors you have used, so followers never see reruns.
- **Rung:** one step of the click-to-revenue ladder. Diagnose at the failing rung only.
- **Runway check:** counting unused anchors before planning a week. Too few means refill the library, not stretch thin.
- **Sales page:** the one page (or flyer) where you make the full case and ask someone to buy.
- **Scarcity:** limited supply. Real scarcity (a calendar, a room, an enforced cap) is fine to state. Fake scarcity is a lie with a screenshot.
- **Scheduler:** an app that publishes your loaded posts to your accounts at set times. Useful later, never required on day one.
- **Scoreboard:** the one page of real numbers (posts, replies, clicks, sign-ups, sales) plus three sentences (Leak, Learning, Dial-in) you fill in on the same day every week.
- **Session:** one AI work period, from opening the chat to closing it. The AI forgets everything between sessions unless it is written in a file it reads.
- **Ship:** to make something public, where a customer can see it. Until then, it is just a draft.
- **Source of record:** the one system you decided in advance to trust when two tools disagree.
- **Standing brief:** the one file the AI reads at the start of every session: who you are, your rules, what good looks like.
- **StoryBrand / BrandScript:** Donald Miller's framework where your customer is the hero and your business is the guide; the BrandScript is the fill-in-the-blanks document it produces.
- **Story file:** your running list of true, dated stories from your own life, 3 to 5 sentences each. Raw material for future posts.
- **Stripe:** a company that processes online payments for you (it keeps a small cut of each sale; check Stripe's pricing page for the current numbers).
- **Substack:** a free platform for publishing essays that also emails them to subscribers.
- **Tag:** a label on a contact in your CRM ("took the quiz," "paid") that triggers the right next step.
- **Template:** a fill-in-the-blank design; only the words change, so every post matches.
- **Terms page:** the written rules of buying from you; your refund promise lives here in the same words as on your sales page. A parent reads it before it goes live.
- **Testimonial:** a customer's own words recommending you. Only usable word-for-word, with their written permission.
- **Text-to-speech (TTS):** software that reads your script out loud, optionally in a clone of your voice.
- **Thank-you page / welcome page:** where people land right after signing up or paying; each one points to the next step.
- **Time Delay:** how long until the buyer gets a result. A bottom lever of the Value Equation, pushed down by building in a fast first win.
- **Token-gated:** a paid download that only opens with a secret expiring link, so non-buyers cannot guess the URL.
- **Tracked link:** a link with tags on the end that report which post and platform each visitor came from.
- **Tracker:** the table with one row per prospect showing who they are and what state they are in (queued, sent, replied, paid, no-buy, no-reply).
- **Transcription:** turning a recorded call into readable text you can search and highlight for exact phrases.
- **The trap (what not to do):** pretending to run a research call while secretly setting up a sales pitch. Banned. A research call is a real research call.
- **Trigger:** the event (usually a tag being added) that starts an automation.
- **Truth file / single source of truth:** the one file where your current prices, links, claims, and promises officially live. Everything else quotes it, and it wins every disagreement.
- **Unsubscribe link:** the legally required "stop emailing me" link; good tools add it automatically.
- **Usage guide:** the table at the bottom of a brand document saying exactly which surface each element goes on.
- **UTM tags (also UTM labels, UTM parameters):** small labels on the end of a link (like `?utm_source=instagram`) that record where a click came from. Spell them identically every time.
- **Value Equation:** Hormozi's way of seeing value: Dream Outcome times Perceived Likelihood, divided by Time Delay times Effort and Sacrifice. Push the top two up and the bottom two down.
- **Verbatim:** their exact words, quoted, not your summary. Paraphrase kills the signal.
- **Verify before publishing:** the flag you write in place of any number or fact you cannot prove yet. It turns honesty into a workflow step.
- **Villain:** the bad idea or fake solution your brand fights, written like a movie character. Never a real person.
- **Voice memo (brand voice):** a half-page cheat sheet of how you talk (words you use, words you ban, how your sentences sound), so everything you publish sounds like you.
- **Voice of the Customer sheet:** the one-page document holding your customers' exact words, sorted into buckets (fears, frustrations, dream, exact phrases, objections, price). The deliverable of the market research chapter and the fuel for every chapter after it.
- **Voice walk:** recording yourself talking (often while walking), then mining the transcript for stories and beliefs.
- **Warm / cold:** how well someone already knows you. Warm people know you or your family; cold people are strangers.
- **Warm list:** your written list of real people who already know you and might care about your offer.
- **Webhook:** an automatic message one app sends another the instant something happens, like Stripe telling your CRM "this person just paid."
- **Win-back:** an honest message to a past customer who drifted away, owning the silence and asking what happened.
- **Yes-call:** the short call you get on the moment someone says "tell me more," where the closing conversation happens.

## The three promises

Before you start, you make three promises. They are the whole playbook in miniature. Break one and none of the rest of this works.

1. **Never fabricate.** No made-up numbers, names, quotes, testimonials, or results. Not once, not small ones, not "just this time."
2. **Verify before publishing.** If you cannot prove a fact yet, you write "verify before publishing" next to it and hold it back until you can.
3. **Parent in the loop.** A parent or guardian sees everything involving money, accounts, or agreements before it happens. Not because you are not capable. Because that is how a real operation manages risk.

### Before you publish anything: the non-negotiables

The three promises above are the heart of it. A few more rules travel with you from day one. You will learn each one in full later, mostly in "Rules of the Game" and "Build It with AI." But you follow them from your very first post, not after you have already published. Read this list once now, then start.

- **Never fabricate.** No made-up numbers, reviews, testimonials, or results. (Promise 1.)
- **Verify before publishing.** If you cannot prove it, you do not post it. (Promise 2.)
- **A trusted adult is in the loop** for money, accounts, recording a call, and any agreement. (Promise 3.)
- **No fake scarcity or urgency.** Only real, enforced limits and real dates. ("Design Your Offer" and "Rules of the Game.")
- **Education, not advice.** You teach and coach. You never promise results or income, and you never give financial, legal, or medical advice. ("Rules of the Game.")
- **Get consent before you tell someone's story.** Written permission, plus a parent's if the person is under 18. ("Rules of the Game.")
- **Credit your sources by name.** ("Rules of the Game.")
- **You are the human gate.** When an AI helps you write, you read and approve everything before it ships, and the AI never invents facts. ("Build It with AI.")
- **If something feels wrong or unsafe, stop and ask your trusted adult.** That is always allowed.

The rules come first. The building comes second. That order is the whole point.
